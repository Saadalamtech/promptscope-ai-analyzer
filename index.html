About PromptScope

Understanding the art and science of prompt engineering

📝

What is Prompt Engineering?

Prompt engineering is the practice of crafting precise, clear, and well-structured inputs to AI models in order to get the most accurate, useful, and relevant responses possible.

A well-engineered prompt is specific about context, defines the task clearly, avoids vague language, and provides enough information for the AI to understand intent without ambiguity.

⚡

Why Prompt Quality Matters

The quality of the output from any AI system is directly proportional to the quality of the input. Vague or poorly structured prompts lead to generic, off-target, or unhelpful responses.

Clear, specific prompts reduce back-and-forth, save time, and dramatically improve the relevance of AI-generated content — whether for writing, coding, analysis, or creative work.

🔬

How PromptScope Evaluates

Clarity (0–100): Measures sentence structure, length, and readability. Shorter, well-structured sentences score higher.

Specificity (0–100): Detects vague filler words ("something", "etc", "maybe") and rewards precise, detail-rich language.

Ambiguity (0–100): Checks for unclear references, missing context clues, and double meanings that could confuse an AI system.

💡

Scoring System

Each dimension is scored from 0 to 100. The overall score is a weighted average:

  • Clarity — 35%
  • Specificity — 40%
  • Ambiguity — 25%

Scores above 75 are considered "Good". Above 90 is "Excellent". Below 50 triggers detailed improvement suggestions.
